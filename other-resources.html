Pro Tips

Apply Smarter, Not Harder

These tips have helped thousands of pre-dents put together stronger, more competitive applications.

⏰
Apply Early

Most dental schools use rolling admissions. Submitting in June vs. October can be the difference between an interview and a rejection. Submit your AADSAS app as soon as it opens.

✍️
Pre-Write Secondaries

Secondary prompts repeat year to year. Use this database to draft your essays before you receive them — so you can submit within 2 weeks of receiving each secondary.

🎯
Tailor Every Essay

Generic essays get filtered out quickly. Research each school's specific mission, programs, and patient population. Name specific faculty, clinics, or initiatives in your "Why Us" essays.

🩺
Stack Dental Hours Early

Aim for 200+ shadowing hours across diverse settings — GP, specialists, and community clinics. Quality matters: describe what you observed, not just how many hours you logged.

📊
Know Your Numbers

The average matriculant has a ~3.5 GPA and a 21+ DAT. Know where you stand and apply to a balanced list of reach, match, and safety schools — don't only apply to top-tier programs.

💬
Prep for MMI Interviews

More schools are moving to MMI format. Practice timed ethical scenarios out loud with a partner. The Bootcamp interview prep service is one of the best options available for structured practice.

Application Timeline

Your Year-by-Year Roadmap

A general guide to when things happen in the dental school application cycle.

April – May
Prepare & Request Letters

Finalize your personal statement, request letters of recommendation, and register for the DAT if you haven't already. Start browsing school lists and pre-writing secondaries using this database.

June
AADSAS Application Opens — Submit ASAP

AADSAS opens in early June. Submit as close to opening day as possible. Rolling admissions mean earlier = better. Don't wait until everything is "perfect."

June – August
Receive & Submit Secondaries

Schools begin sending secondary applications after your AADSAS is verified. Aim to submit each secondary within 2 weeks of receiving it. Use your pre-written drafts to move fast.

September – December
Interview Season

Interview invitations roll out from September onward. Prepare using mock interviews, MMI practice, and the Bootcamp interview coaching service. Send thank-you notes after each interview.

December – April
Decisions & Acceptance

Acceptance letters begin arriving in December. If waitlisted, send a letter of interest to your top schools. Hold deposits are typically due by April 30.

It typically includes school-specific essays and sometimes additional forms or fees. Not all schools send secondaries to every applicant — some are sent to everyone, while others screen applicants first.

A balanced list includes reach schools (GPA/DAT above your stats), match schools (your stats match their averages), and safety schools (your stats exceed their averages). Applying to fewer than 10 schools significantly increases your risk of not being accepted in a given cycle.
